Pain points we solve for both sides

Traveler pain points:

  • Expensive intercity rides with inconsistent reliability.
  • No structured space to negotiate safely and transparently.
  • Poor visibility on future ride availability for planning.
  • Low trust in confirmation and post-booking updates.

Driver pain points:

  • Unpaid dead-mile returns with empty seats.
  • Difficulty discovering matching demand in time.
  • No unified system for offers, counters, and proof flow.
  • Low profitability visibility per route cycle.

Maicoins economics, referrals, and tier growth

  • Maicoins as savings engine: users can reduce platform fee within configured limits, driving repeat usage.
  • Referral code model: each user gets a unique referral identity, rewards unlock as referred users complete valid ride activity.
  • Tier-linked loyalty: quality engagement and completed activity compound long-term wallet value.
  • Negligible entry cost: service usage fee is intentionally kept light to support large-scale adoption.

Why MaiRide can outperform traditional alternatives

  • Empty-leg optimization first: monetizes trips that already happen, instead of forcing fresh dispatch dependence.
  • Two-way negotiation loop: fair-market pricing between real people with continuous visibility.
  • Operational clarity: booking, payment, support tickets, and records all live in one ecosystem.
  • Sustainability edge: better seat utilization means lower avoidable fuel burn per paid ride.
✅ Every matched empty leg reduces avoidable road and fuel wastage.
